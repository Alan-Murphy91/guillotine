5 Reasons Why Everybody Should Lift Weights


A common misconception from affectionately named “Non-lifters” is that anyone who lifts weights is an overblown, aggressive Arnold Schwarzenegger wannabe but what I hope this article makes you realise is that everybody no matter what age or gender can benefit from weight training. The benefits of resistance training are universal to every demographic, the stereotype of weight lifting being exclusive to aggressive sweaty men who are angry at the world and now take their anger out in the gym via excessive grunting is funny but simply not true, everybody from teenagers to grannies should be involved in some form of resistance exercise and here’s why:


  • Improved Body Composition

    Everybody wants to look good naked, resistance training will improve your body composition helping you to lose fat and gain lean muscle mass which is not only advantageous to your health and performance but also for your confidence. There is nothing wrong with wanting to swap love handles for abs “being comfortable in the skin you’re in” is important but so is striving to have a healthy body. You can do all the fancy dance classes you want shaking your limbs around like a lunatic wont transform your physique, weight training will.

  • Improved performance

    There is no doubt that resistance training is a key aspect to improving performance. All elite athletes incorporate some form of resistance training into their programs. The type of resistance training can vary massively depending on specific sports (Rugby Vs Cycling for example). Use a weight training protocol that is specific to your sport but there is no getting around it resistance training = increased performance. Sport is all about creating an advantage; resistance training is an advantage that cannot be neglected.

  • Lift weights and live longer

    Ireland has an ageing demographic so the issue of age related muscle wastage is a big concern for a large portion of our population. The bad news is sarcopenia (Age related muscle wastage) will affect you as you age but the good news is unlike a lot of other age related illnesses this can be negated massively and reduced with the use of resistance exercise. Making your body work by providing your muscles with a stimulus to keep them active and therefore avoiding muscle wastage is the most vital step in avoiding sarcopenia. It is never too late to start a resistance training program a study by (Fiatarone et al) showed that even frail older adults aged 90+ massively benefited from resistance training in regards to reducing muscle loss and frailty.

  • All genders Benefit

    I often hear the myth that women shouldn’t lift weights because they will become bulky or manly. Women do not have adequate amounts of testosterone to gain the level of muscle mass possessed by their male counterparts. Ladies of Ireland (I say Ireland because this myth died a long time ago in most countries yet is still very common here) do not fret lifting weights is the best thing you can do for your body. Weight training will change your body but change it for the better you won’t become a hairy, deep voiced wildebeest.

  • Improved Mental strength

    Not only does a resistance training programme benefit you physically but it also will greatly improve your mental strength and resilience which directly translates to other areas of your life. Grinding out the last rep, taking your muscles to failure and progressing are examples of things which will benefit you far beyond the walls of the gym and are traits which can be used in other areas of your life.
